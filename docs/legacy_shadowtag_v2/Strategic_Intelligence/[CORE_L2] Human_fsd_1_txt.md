4. Who Would Buy It?
   • Primary Buyers:

5. Current Cost of “Fixing” Contracts in the U.S.
   1. Wasted Spend on Poor Contracting Practices
      • According to IACCM, organizations lose on average 9% of contract value due to errors, ambiguities, and non‑compliance [6].
      • On a $100 M contract book, that’s $9 M in avoidable costs.
   2. Overall Legal Spend on Contract Corrections
      • Legal departments allocate 20–30% of their time to remedial contract work (addenda, amendments, dispute avoidance) [7].
      • With the U.S. corporate legal services market at $300 B, even 10% rework implies $30 B per year spent on “back‑end” corrections.
   3. Per‑Contract Economics
      • Average single commercial contract takes ~40 hours of attorney + paralegal time over its lifecycle [8].
      • At blended $300/hr, that’s $12 000 per contract—of which ~30% ($3 600) is spent on post‑signing fixes.

Bottom line: Billions of dollars are wasted annually on misdrafts, misunderstandings, and post‑signing amendments—precisely the inefficiency a transcript‑to‑contract LLM could reduce.

Why no direct end‑to‑end player yet? 1. Technical Gap
• Meeting tools excel at transcription, but rarely link output into a legal‑grade drafting engine.
• Contract platforms generate docs, but lack robust, synchronized ASR pipelines. 2. Regulatory & Liability Concerns

Adjacent Approaches to Watch
• Plug‑in Bundling:
• Some startups are experimenting with Zoom/Teams plug‑ins that trigger a “turn transcript into a first‑draft” action—usually still requiring manual clause selection afterward.
