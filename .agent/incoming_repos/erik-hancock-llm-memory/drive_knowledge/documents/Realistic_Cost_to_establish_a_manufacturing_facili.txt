🔧 Notable Technical & Regulatory Considerations
1. Ozone Handling & Emissions
You will need indoor ozone monitoring systems, emergency shutdown protocols, and potentially EPA reporting.
OSHA limits ozone exposure to 0.1 ppm over 8 hours, so the design must demonstrate containment and neutralization (which your activated carbon filter helps with).
May require independent lab validation of ozone scrubbing efficacy post-carbon filter.
2. FDA Regulatory Classification
This will likely be a Class II device due to active sterilization features and patient proximity.
You’ll need 510(k) clearance, meaning bench testing, biocompatibility, emissions data, and real-world simulations.
3. Cleanroom Assembly for Filters & Mask Components
PAPR-style masks require strict assembly protocols, especially for:
Filter sealing
Flow path control
Electronics (e.g., for ozone generation and battery packs)
