Intel QuickSync is integrated into your CPU’s iGPU (integrated graphics), so there’s nothing to download — it’s already in the silicon. What you do need is:

How QuickSync Works
	•	It’s a hardware video encoder/decoder built into Intel CPUs with iGPUs.
	•	It drastically speeds up H.264, H.265 (HEVC), and sometimes VP9/AV1 video encoding compared to software encoding (CPU-only).
	•	It reduces CPU load during tasks like streaming, video editing, or transcoding in apps like OBS, Premiere, or HandBrake.

How to Enable / Integrate
	1.	BIOS:
	•	Ensure iGPU is enabled (iGPU Multi-Monitor = Enabled in BIOS, which you already have from your screenshots).
	•	Even if you have a discrete GPU, keeping the iGPU enabled lets QuickSync work in the background.
	2.	Drivers:
	•	Install the latest Intel Graphics drivers (these contain the QuickSync codec support).
	•	Windows Update may install basic drivers, but Intel’s site has the latest.
	3.	Software Integration:
	•	In OBS, HandBrake, Adobe, DaVinci Resolve, etc., choose Intel QSV (QuickSync Video) as your encoder.
	•	You can still use your NVIDIA/AMD GPU for rendering/AI workloads while QuickSync handles video encoding in parallel.

When to Use
	•	Streaming: Offloads encoding from CPU/GPU → smoother gameplay and better FPS.
	•	Video Editing/Transcoding: Huge time savings on render/export.
	•	Power Efficiency: Uses less power and heat than CPU-only encoding.

👉 Since you already have a discrete GPU, best setup is:
	•	Keep monitor on the GPU for graphics performance.
	•	Leave iGPU enabled in BIOS.
	•	Use QuickSync just for encoding tasks → frees CPU + GPU for everything else.
