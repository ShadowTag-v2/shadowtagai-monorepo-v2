## II. KERNEL PROMPTING FRAMEWORK
**"Successful prompts follow 6 consistent patterns."**

### K - Keep it Simple
*   **Goal**: One clear goal per prompt.
*   *Bad*: "Help me with X and Y."
*   *Good*: "Write a technical tutorial on X."

### E - Easy to Verify
*   **Goal**: Clear success criteria.
*   *Good*: "Include 3 code examples."

### R - Reproducible Results
*   **Goal**: No "current trends". Specific versions.

### N - Narrow Scope
*   **Goal**: One prompt = One goal. Split complex tasks.

### E - Explicit Constraints
*   **Goal**: Tell AI what NOT to do.
*   *Good*: "No external libraries. No functions over 20 lines."

### L - Logical Structure
1.  **Context**: "You are the Wealth Strategist..."
2.  **Task**: "Analyze this revenue model."
3.  **Constraints**: "Focus on leverage. No theory."
4.  **Format**: "Executive Summary + Direct Challenge."
