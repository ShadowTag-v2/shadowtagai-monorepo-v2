# CounselConduit: The Attorney Experience & Aggregated Billing
**Designing the Turnkey "Reseller" Proxy**

## 1. The Core Problem: Technical Attrition
Lawyers are not technical operators. If your platform requires an attorney to generate API keys from OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google Cloud, plug them into a dashboard, and manage separate billing accounts across five different tech companies, **churn will approach 100%.**

The product must be completely turnkey. The lawyer wants to push one button, text the link, and see a billable time entry in their practice management software the next morning. They do not want to know what an API is.

## 2. The Solution: The "Aggregated Umbrella"
**CounselConduit acts as a master aggregator/reseller.**

We maintain massive, centralized Enterprise API accounts with OpenAI (GPT), Anthropic (Claude), Google (Gemini), and Perplexity. The lawyer does not subscribe to these individually. Instead, they subscribe *only* to CounselConduit.

We are the ISP (Internet Service Provider); the LLMs are just different websites we let them access.

### 2.1 The Attorney's UX Flow (Zero Friction)
1. **Sign Up:** The attorney enters their credit card and firm name ($149/mo access fee).
2. **Dashboard Overview:** They see a simple interface: "Active Client Links," "Unbilled Time to Review," and "Monthly Compute Bill."
3. **Provisioning a Link:** To onboard a client, the attorney clicks "Generate Link."
    * A modal pops up asking: *"Which AI tools can this client use?"* with simple toggle switches for Claude, GPT, Gemini, and Grok (default is all ON).
    * They type the client's name/case number (for billing attribution mapping to Clio).
    * They click "Text Client."
4. **That is it.** The technical interaction is entirely finished.

### 2.2 How the Lawyer Interacts with the Output
The following morning, the lawyer logs into the dashboard to review the "Nightly Digest."
* They click on *John Smith (Case #102)*.
* They are presented with a clean transcript of the exact conversation John Smith had with Claude the night before.
* They simply read it, perhaps check a few boxes the client flagged, and click **"Push 1.2 hours to Clio."**
* The technical loop is closed. The revenue has been securely captured.

## 3. The Attorney-Facing Billing Model
Because we maintain the master API subscriptions, we handle all the raw usage tracking and backend costs. The lawyer receives **one unified monthly invoice** from CounselConduit that contains two sections:

1. **SaaS Access Fee:** $149/mo (per attorney seat). This covers the platform, the VPN proxy infrastructure, the Clio integration, and the Magic Link system.
2. **Compute Pass-Through (The "AI Toll"):** This is where CounselConduit aggregates the raw API usage of that attorney's clients across all models (Claude, GPT, etc.), and bills it to the attorney’s credit card.
    * *Margin Strategy:* By acting as the aggregator, CounselConduit can mark up the wholesale API cost by 20%-30% (e.g., if a client burns $1.00 of Claude tokens, we bill the attorney $1.25).
    * *The Pitch to the Lawyer:* "We handle all AI subscriptions. You reimburse us for your clients' exact usage, just like you reimburse Westlaw or a court reporter fee."

## 4. Why This Works Perfectly with "The Magic Link"
By acting as the singular aggregator, you reinforce the exact legal phrasing in the Magic Link:
> *"The caveats being, it has to be my subscription, the answers belong to me..."*

Legally, it *is* the attorney’s subscription. They are paying CounselConduit for an enterprise umbrella account that spans all major models. The client is legally accessing the firm's procured resources. If the client were bringing their own OpenAI ChatGPT Plus account ($20/mo), the privilege argument instantly shatters into a million pieces because the firm has no ownership over the interaction.

## 5. Exit Play Refinement: The Billing Aggregator
By controlling the financial pipe to the LLMs, CounselConduit’s valuation climbs even higher.
* You are not just a SaaS tool; you are the **exclusive clearinghouse** for AI usage within the legal sector.
* When you sell to a strategic acquirer like Clio, you aren't just selling them software MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue). You are selling them a **metered tollbridge** that skims a 20% margin off every single AI token generated by 400,000+ law firms.
* That transaction volume alone makes the $300M+ valuation floor mathematically bulletproof.
