## 3. Strategic Trade-Offs: The Architectural Decision

### 3.1 Synchronous (Judge #6) vs Asynchronous (Agent Governance)

Branch B implements **synchronous enforcement** (p99≤90ms):
- ✅ Immediate blocking (nothing bad happens)
- ✅ Deterministic (same input = same output)
- ✅ Low latency (<90ms)
- ❌ Limited context (simple rules only)
- ❌ No learning (static logic)
- ❌ Higher cost per decision ($0.003 vs $0.0003)

Branch C proposes **asynchronous agent governance** (2-5 seconds):
- ✅ Deep context awareness (full situation analysis)
- ✅ Natural language policies (rapid updates)
- ✅ Learning capability (improves from experience)
- ✅ 10x cheaper ($0.0003-0.0012 per decision)
- ❌ Eventual consistency (2-5 second window)
- ❌ Non-deterministic (LLM probabilistic)
- ❌ Hallucination risk (confident but wrong)

**The hybrid solution (recommended):**

```
98% of requests → OPA rules (<10ms, deterministic)
2% of requests → Agent reasoning (2-5s, context-aware)
Weighted average latency: ~100ms
```

**Financial impact of hybrid:**

| Decision Type | Volume | Cost (Sync) | Cost (Hybrid) | Savings |
|---------------|--------|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Simple (98%) | 9.8M/mo | $29,400 | $0 (OPA negligible) | $29,400 |
| Complex (2%) | 200K/mo | $600 | $240 (agents) | $360 |
| **Total** | **10M/mo** | **$30,000** | **$240** | **$29,760/mo** |

**Annual savings:** $357K by switching to hybrid architecture
