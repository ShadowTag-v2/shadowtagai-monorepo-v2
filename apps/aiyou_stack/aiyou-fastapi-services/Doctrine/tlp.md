# TROOP LEADING PROCEDURES (TLP)

_Reference: FM 7-8, Infantry Rifle Platoon and Squad_

## 1. Receive the Mission

The leader receives the mission in the form of a warning order (WARNO), an operation order (OPORD), or a fragmentary order (FRAGO). The leader immediately begins to analyze the mission using the factors of METT-TC (Mission, Enemy, Terrain, Troops, Time, Civil Considerations).

## 2. Issue a Warning Order (WARNO)

The leader provides initial instructions to subordinates to allow them to begin preparation. The WARNO includes:

- Mission statement

- Time and place for issuing the OPORD

- Units participating

- Specific tasks

- Timeline

## 3. Make a Tentative Plan

The leader develops an estimate of the situation to form a tentative plan. This involves:

- Mission Analysis

- Course of Action (COA) Development

- COA Analysis

- COA Comparison

- COA Selection

## 4. Initiate Movement

The unit may need to move to an assembly area, battle position, or line of departure while the leader is still planning. This step can occur at any time during the TLP.

## 5. Conduct Reconnaissance

The leader must confirm the plan through personal reconnaissance. If time or circumstances do not permit, the leader must use map reconnaissance or other means (e.g., drone feeds, previous reports).

## 6. Complete the Plan

Based on the results of the reconnaissance, the leader refines the tentative plan into a final plan. This includes preparing the OPORD, overlays, and other coordinating instructions.

## 7. Issue the Order

The leader issues the OPORD to subordinates. This should be done visually (using terrain models or maps) and clearly. The leader ensures understanding through backbriefs.

## 8. Supervise and Refine

The leader supervises preparations (rehearsals, inspections) and refines the plan as necessary. Rehearsals are critical to ensure everyone understands their role.
