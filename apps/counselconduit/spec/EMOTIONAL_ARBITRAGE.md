# Emotional Arbitrage — The Business Engine

> "The gap between what the AI costs to provide and what emotional security is worth."

## Definition
Emotional arbitrage is the core economic mechanism of CounselConduit. We charge premium SaaS prices ($149-$999/mo) for services that cost us ~$0.02/query to deliver, because the value proposition is not compute — it's **emotional safety**.

## The Math
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| LLM inference per query | ~$0.02 |
| Infrastructure per user/mo | ~$3-5 |
| Total cost per user/mo | ~$5-8 |
| Charge per user/mo | $149-$999 |
| **Gross margin** | **85-95%** |

## Why Clients Pay
1. **Safety**: "Someone smart has my back 24/7"
2. **Clarity**: "I finally understand what this legal letter means"
3. **Agency**: "I know my options and I'm choosing, not being dragged"
4. **Speed**: "I don't have to wait 3 weeks for my attorney to return my call"
5. **Privacy**: "Nobody has to know I'm researching this"

## Why This Works (Behavioral Economics)
- **Loss aversion**: Legal problems trigger fear of loss → willingness to pay for safety
- **Anchoring**: Traditional attorney rates ($300-800/hr) make $149/mo feel like a steal
- **Endowment effect**: Once clients feel "my AI attorney", switching cost is psychological
- **Sunk cost**: The more sessions they complete, the more invested they feel

## Invisible Meter Design
The session must feel infinite, not transactional.

**Rules**:
1. Session timer is hidden — never show duration
2. No idle prompts — Dead-Man's Switch architecture handles cleanup
3. Ambient micro-animations create aliveness
4. Progressive depth — start reassurance, surface intelligence gradually
5. "One More Thing" cadence at end of every response
6. Warm close — "You're doing the right thing" (never "goodbye")

**Target**: Avg session > 45 minutes
