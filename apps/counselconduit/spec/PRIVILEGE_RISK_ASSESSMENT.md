## Core Question: What if Heppner is Narrowed or Overturned?

### Bottom Line
The business case weakens somewhat but **does not disappear**.

The moat is not just "privilege after Heppner." It is also:
1. Avoiding waiver under ordinary third-party disclosure rules
2. Reducing embarrassing/harmful discoverable artifacts
3. Creating a cleaner counsel-directed record
4. Moving client activity from uncontrolled public tools into managed legal environments

## How Frequently Do Litigants' Web Searches Actually Get Discovered?

**Honest answer**: There does not appear to be a reliable nationwide empirical statistic.

### Civil Cases
Browser/search history is discoverable **sometimes, not routinely**.

- Governed by **proportionality under Rule 26(b)(1)**
- Parties do not automatically get to rummage through opponent's full digital life
- Request must be relevant and proportional to the needs of the case
- Most likely sought when it goes to: **intent, notice, alibi, mitigation, damages,
  alternate causes, misappropriation, or spoliation**
- ABA e-discovery guidance emphasizes proportionality and cost/burden limits on ESI

> **Working rule**: Not common as a default artifact, but common enough in the right
> fact pattern that you should assume it can surface.

### Criminal Cases
Generally **more common** than in civil cases.

- Law enforcement obtains digital evidence through warrants, device seizures,
  and forensic review with probable cause
- **Riley v. California**: Police generally need a warrant to search digital info
  on seized phone — but that's a warrant *requirement*, not a ban
- DOJ manual: Electronic evidence can be obtained when reasonably needed;
  searches require tailored procedures and filters
- NIJ: Internet searches can be used to show **intent or premeditation**

## California Privilege Statutes (Independent of Heppner)

California defines confidential lawyer-client communications as those not disclosed
to unnecessary third persons. Privilege is waived where a significant part is
voluntarily disclosed, **but** privilege is preserved for electronic communications
merely because electronic intermediaries store or transmit them.

**Implication**: Even without Heppner, the central architectural move makes sense:
keep client fact development inside the lawyer-directed system, not in consumer tools.
