### The Problem

**83% of Americans who need a lawyer never hire one.** Not because lawyers don't exist — but because the intake process is broken. Potential clients call at 2:00 AM in a panic, get voicemail, call three other firms, and whoever picks up first wins. The lawyer who should have won the case is left with a dead lead and an unreturned call.

Meanwhile, the lawyers who DO answer are drowning. **94% of attorneys report stress.** **28% suffer depression.** Not from the law — from the *counseling*. Their license says "Attorney and *Counselor* at Law," but no one trained them to absorb human trauma at scale.

### The Solution

**KovelAI is a 1-Click Appliance that law firms install to capture, triage, and bill client intake — 24/7 — while maintaining absolute attorney-client privilege.**

- **The Client** gets a *safe pressure-release valve* — an AI counselor that receives their panic, structures it, and sends a clean brief to the lawyer.
- **The Lawyer** gets an *asynchronous buffer* — no more 2:00 AM calls: a pre-triaged, pre-billed case file appears in their dashboard.
- **The Firm** gets *structured, paid intake* — every interaction is monetized from the first message.

### The Legal Moat: *U.S. v. Heppner*

In February 2026, J. Paul Engelmayer (S.D.N.Y.) ruled that an attorney whose client used a consumer AI tool (ChatGPT) **lost privilege** over those communications. *Heppner* created a binary market:

| Before Heppner | After Heppner |
|---|---|
| Clients use ChatGPT freely | Any non-firm AI = waived privilege |
| No billing on client research | First-mover billing advantage |
| AI is a nice feature | Privileged AI is a **legal necessity** |

**KovelAI is the only platform that routes AI through the firm's own infrastructure**, making every query privileged under the Kovel Doctrine (accountant-CPA analogy extended to AI agents).
