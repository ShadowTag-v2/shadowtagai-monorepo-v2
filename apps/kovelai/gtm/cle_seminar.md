### Seminar Description

This CLE seminar examines the immediate practical impact of *U.S. v. Heppner* (S.D.N.Y., Feb. 2026) on attorney-client privilege in the age of generative AI. Attendees will learn:

1. **The Heppner Rule**: Why client use of consumer AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) now creates a privilege waiver under the work-product doctrine.

2. **The Kovel Doctrine Solution**: How the IRS accountant-privilege analogy (*U.S. v. Kovel*, 2d Cir. 1961) extends to AI agents operating under attorney direction.

3. **Practical Infrastructure**: How to deploy a privileged AI proxy within your firm's infrastructure to capture, triage, and bill AI-assisted client intake.

4. **Revenue Capture**: How to monetize the 83% of potential clients who currently self-research on free AI tools and never call a lawyer.

### Key Takeaways (Handout)

#### The 3 Requirements for AI Privilege Under Kovel:

1. **Attorney Direction**: The AI tool must operate under the express direction of licensed counsel (not the client directly).
2. **Infrastructure Control**: The firm must control the AI infrastructure — self-hosted or firm-managed cloud with no third-party data retention.
3. **Engagement Nexus**: The AI interaction must occur within the scope of an existing or contemplated attorney-client engagement.

#### The Risk Matrix:

| Client Action | Privileged? | Revenue? | Malpractice Risk? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Client uses ChatGPT on their own | ❌ | ❌ | ⚠️ If you knew and didn't warn |
| Client uses firm's KovelAI portal | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ Fully protected |
| Client emails ChatGPT output to lawyer | ❌ | ❌ | ⚠️ Discoverable |
| Client uses KovelAI + firm reviews | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ Gold standard |
