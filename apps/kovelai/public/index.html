Zero-Access Architecture

We Cannot See Your Client's Data. By Design.

KovelAI is built on a strict data isolation architecture. Your firm's data and your client's research never mix with ours — or anyone else's.

Per-Firm Cloud Storage

Every law firm receives its own isolated Google Cloud Storage bucket with a unique, cryptographically-generated namespace. Client transcripts, uploaded documents, and session artifacts are stored exclusively in your firm's bucket — physically separated from every other firm on the platform. We use GCP's IAM policies to enforce tenant boundaries at the infrastructure level, not just the application level.

Encryption at Every Layer

Data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3), at rest (AES-256 via Google-managed keys), and can optionally use Customer-Managed Encryption Keys (CMEK) on Enterprise plans — meaning even Google cannot decrypt your data without your key. Firestore documents holding session metadata use the same per-tenant isolation with field-level access controls.

KovelAI Staff Cannot Access Your Data

Our Cloud Run services authenticate via dedicated per-firm service accounts that are scoped exclusively to that firm's resources. KovelAI engineering and operations staff have zero standing access to client data buckets. There are no admin backdoors, no shared database tables, and no batch jobs that read across tenant boundaries. Audit logs via Cloud Logging record every data access event and are available to your firm on request.

Automatic 30-Day GDPR Purge

Client ephemeral research sessions are automatically deleted after 30 days via a Cloud Scheduler job. Attorney-side transcripts and Kovel attestation receipts are retained per your firm's configured retention policy. When a client relationship ends, a single API call triggers a complete, verified data deletion across all storage layers — with a cryptographic deletion receipt for your records.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Kovel doctrine and why does it matter for AI?

The Kovel doctrine extends attorney-client privilege to communications with third-party experts retained by counsel. After United States v. Heppner (S.D.N.Y., Feb. 2026), client internet searches became discoverable. KovelAI creates a privileged portal so client AI research remains non-discoverable — backed by cryptographic attestation receipts for each session.

How does KovelAI protect client-attorney privilege for AI searches?

Every client query is routed through your firm's privileged Kovel session. KovelAI generates an HMAC-SHA256 hash per session proving privileged communication, stores immutable transcripts visible only to the supervising attorney, and auto-wipes the client's ephemeral portal after inactivity. No export, no copy, no print.

Which AI models does KovelAI support?

KovelAI routes queries to Gemini, Claude, ChatGPT, and Grok through a unified privileged interface. Clients choose the model best suited to their task. All queries are routed through your firm's secure session with full citation trails.

Can KovelAI staff access my client's data?

No. Every firm gets an isolated Google Cloud Storage bucket with unique IAM policies. Our Cloud Run services use dedicated per-firm service accounts. There are zero admin backdoors, zero shared databases, and zero standing access. Every data access attempt is logged and auditable.

How do attorneys get paid through KovelAI?

Clients subscribe to your firm's AI portal with their credit card via Stripe. Funds flow directly to your firm's Stripe account — you get paid before the first query runs. Your review sessions are compensated professional oversight, not unpaid inbox labor. It's a new, recurring revenue line.

What happens to data after a case concludes?

KovelAI runs an automatic 30-day GDPR-compliant purge cycle via Google Cloud Scheduler. After the retention window, all client data is cryptographically deleted with verifiable deletion receipts. Enterprise plans offer configurable retention periods to match your firm's policies.
