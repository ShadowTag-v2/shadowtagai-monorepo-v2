## [COLD OPEN — 0:00-1:30]

**ERIK:** "On February 10th, 2026, Judge Katherine Forrest of the Southern
District of New York issued a ruling that will change how every lawyer in
America uses AI. *United States versus Heppner*. And in that ruling, she
drew a line.

On one side: AI research that's privileged and protected from discovery.
On the other: AI research that opposing counsel can subpoena, read, and
use against your client.

Most lawyers don't know which side they're on right now.

I'm Erik Hanson. I'm the founder of KovelAI, and this is *The Autonomous
Law Firm* — a podcast about building law practices that use AI not as a
toy, but as infrastructure.

In this first episode, we're going to break down exactly what Heppner means
for your firm, why ChatGPT's terms of service are a ticking time bomb for
privilege claims, and what the 'Kovel doctrine' — a 60-year-old tax case —
has to do with any of this."

---

## [SEGMENT 1: THE HEPPNER PROBLEM — 1:30-7:00]

**ERIK:** "Let me set the scene. It's 2025. According to a Thomson Reuters
survey, 78% of AmLaw 200 firms have adopted some form of AI. Associates
are using ChatGPT to draft motions. Partners are using Claude to summarize
depositions. Paralegals are using Perplexity to research case law.

And every single one of those interactions is potentially discoverable.

Why? Because when you type a client question into ChatGPT, you've
transmitted that information to a third party — OpenAI. And under the
traditional privilege analysis, voluntary disclosure to a third party
waives privilege.

*Talk about specific Heppner ruling details*
*Discuss the Kovel doctrine (United States v. Kovel, 296 F.2d 918, 2d Cir. 1961)*
*Explain how KovelAI applies the Kovel framework to AI intermediaries*"

Let me walk you through what 'zero-data routing' means...
