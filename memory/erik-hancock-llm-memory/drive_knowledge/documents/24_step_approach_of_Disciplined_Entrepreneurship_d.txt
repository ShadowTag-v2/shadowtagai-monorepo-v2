Of course, here is the 24-step approach of "Disciplined Entrepreneurship" taught at MIT:
This framework is broken down into 6 overarching themes to guide you through the process of building a successful startup.
Who is Your Customer?
1. Market Segmentation: Start by brainstorming a wide variety of potential customers and markets for your idea.
2. Select a Beachhead Market: Choose one market segment to focus on and win over first. This will be your entry point.
3. Build an End User Profile: Create a detailed profile of your ideal customer within the beachhead market.
4. Calculate the Total Addressable Market (TAM) Size for the Beachhead Market: Estimate the total revenue you could generate if you captured 100% of your beachhead market.
5. Profile the Persona for the Beachhead Market: Go beyond the user profile and create a detailed story about one specific, real person who represents your ideal customer.

What Can You Do For Your Customer?
6. Full Life Cycle Use Case: Map out how your persona will use your product, from the moment they become aware of it to the end of its life.
7. High-Level Product Specification: Create a visual representation and description of your product.
8. Quantify the Value Proposition: Clearly state the tangible benefits your product will provide to the customer.
9. Identify Your Next 10 Customers: Find at least 10 potential customers who are similar to your persona.
10. Define Your Core: Determine the one thing you do better than anyone else that is difficult to copy.
11. Chart Your Competitive Position: Visually represent where your product stands in relation to competitors based on the persona's top two priorities.

How Does Your Customer Acquire Your Product?
12. Determine the Customer's Decision-Making Unit (DMU): Identify everyone who has a say in the decision to purchase your product.
13. Map the Process to Acquire a Paying Customer: Outline all the steps a customer goes through to buy your product.

How Do You Make Money Off Your Product?
14. Calculate the Total Addressable Market Size for Follow-on Markets: After conquering your beachhead market, identify adjacent markets you can expand into.
15. Design a Business Model: Determine how you will capture a portion of the value you create for your customer.
16. Set Your Pricing Framework: Decide how much you will charge for your product and why.
17. Calculate the Lifetime Value (LTV) of an Acquired Customer: Estimate the total profit you'll make from an average customer over the entire course of your relationship.

How Do You Design & Build Your Product?
18. Map the Sales Process to Acquire a Customer: Detail the steps your sales team will take to land a new customer.
19. Calculate the Cost of Customer Acquisition (COCA): Figure out how much it will cost to acquire a new customer.
20. Identify Key Assumptions: List the most critical assumptions your business plan relies on.
21. Test Key Assumptions: Design and run experiments to validate or invalidate your key assumptions.
22. Define the Minimum Viable Business Product (MVBP): Determine the smallest possible product you can build that delivers value to the customer and allows you to test your assumptions.
23. Show That "the Dogs Will Eat the Dog Food": Get your MVBP into the hands of customers and gather feedback to prove they want it.

How Do You Scale Your Business?
24. Develop a Product Plan: Create a roadmap for the future development of your product.
